Concept

I believe theScore’s biggest competitive advantage is its ability to “personalize” a user’s consumption of sports. In that sense, fantasy sports is an ideal domain to have. The mix of luck, skill, & timing of a team all create an addicting multiplayer experience.

That is the primary benefit to this proposed feature, because a fantasy player has a personal stake in the events at hand (skin in the game).

The layout to my proposed fantasy section showcases the fundamental measures of success for any fantasy player: a live team feed, schedule, daily performers, and season rankings.

A landing page for a "Fantasy" section

Features

Given how fundamental these metrics are, this section can be usable even for first time users. Observing the daily performers for example allows discovery of "under the radar" talent. Further, looking at the season-long rankings allows users to observe an unbiased ranking of players.

The key to creating an addicting experience is based on the following loop involved with monitoring a fantasy section.

The usage loop involved for fantasy players

All the added features serve to reinforce that loop and keep the user's focus on the players they "own". Since performances changes daily, each section can be organized by position, averages, or totals depending on what the user wants to know.
