Concept

Elysian's cafes are welcoming environments. It's small, full of greenery, and surrounded by warm pastel colors. I redesigned the website to reflect that feeling. I changed the layout of the home page, particularly the language and segmentation of elements, while also considering its compatibility with mobile devices.

The original Elysian home page (https://elysiancoffee.com/)

Language

I added a few blocks to the home page to communicate more of Elysian's offerings. For one, I included more calls to action and brand statements that are all welcoming in tone. These messages are embedded with images to show things a user might see when walking in their cafes such as tables, boxes of product, or an assortment of merchandise.

Blocks added to the home page--the brand statement, wholesale inquiries, and subscription plans

Placement

The primary change here was the navigation bar. I moved it to the top to a more conventional position to make room for "previews" of the sections listed on the menu. I thought that there was more to Elysian than just their product, so I incorporated as much of those factors into one page.

The shop is minimized now to a third of the screen, modified so that the user does not have to scroll through a seemingly endless list of products on the first visit of the site.

A section to showcase the shop's primary product and preview other items with a background image.

Then, I split two blocks to show where someone could enjoy their coffee: either at one of their four locations or your own home. The cafe photos showcase each location (linked to Google Maps upon a click), while the second box shows various subscription plans for their coffee delivery service.

Two squares showing the "locations" a user can drink Elysian coffee.

Lastly, the final section is for wholesale goods. Just below the locations is an image to show Elysian's reputable partners. This is important to establish credibility for the following call to action prompting the user to partner with them. Note again here that the prompt is phrased as if to welcome someone in an existing community of coffee lovers, instead of a corporate relationship.

Show the quality of partnerships first, then ask if they would want to join that group.
